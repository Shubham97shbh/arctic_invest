Abstracts

English Abstract

The invention relates to an ex-vivo method for the prognosis of metastasis in prostate cancer, which comprises determining the expression level of at least one of the following genes: NRP-1, MFAP4, NRIP3, THBS2, TNF, EDN1, EBF1 and GALNT16, in a biological sample containing stromal cells from a primary prostate cancer tumour, and establishing if the expression of genes NRP-1, MFAP4, NRIP3, THBS2, TNF, EDN1 and EBF1 is increased or if the expression of the gene GALNT16 is reduced, which correlates with the prognosis of metastasis in prostate cancer. The invention also relates to a kit for making a prognosis of metastasis in prostate cancer, which comprises means for quantifying the products of genes NRP-1, MFAP4, NRIP3, THBS2, TNF, EDN1, EBF1 and GALNT16 in a sample comprising prostate stromal cells.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION
Prostate cancer (PCa) is the second leading cause of death from cancer in men
in Chile

States, deaths

and more
effective methods are urged to predict the degree of aggressiveness of PCa,
and thus

preventing
the development of metastasis.
Research on the stromal role in the progression of PCa arises due to this
need. The
cancer cells are not isolated from their environment, but interact strongly
with the tumor
micro-structure or stroma, that is to say, the cells of the organ of origin of
the tumor, in
this case the prostate. The inventors have observed that the cells that
accompany the
tumor cells in the primary tumor, in this case in the prostate gland,
participate in the
ability of these to develop metastases.
Based on these important observations, the inventors developed the method of
the

PCa,
